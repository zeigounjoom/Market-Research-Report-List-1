<p><p>Thyristor Controlled Reactors (TCR) are essential components in power systems, used to manage reactive power and stabilize voltage levels. The market for TCRs is segmented into two main types: reactors and thyristor valves. Reactors adjust electrical current flow, enhancing system efficiency by providing additional inductance. Thyristor valves, on the other hand, control and regulate the power delivery by rapidly switching on and off, thereby allowing precise control over reactive power compensation in transmission and distribution networks.</p></p>
