<p><p>The sequential logic circuit market encompasses three main types: event-driven, clock-driven, and pulse circuits. Event-driven circuits respond to specific input changes, enabling dynamic operations without a clock signal, suited for asynchronous systems. Clock-driven circuits rely on regular clock pulses for synchronization, ensuring predictable timing and state changes, ideal for synchronous designs. Pulse circuits operate based on narrow voltage pulses, facilitating high-speed operations and short-duration tasks. Each type caters to distinct applications, influencing market demand and technology innovation.</p></p>
